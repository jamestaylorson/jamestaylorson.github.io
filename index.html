Helping Amazon export their prime processes

GfK

Amazon approached GfK to help test a new solution they had developed that allowed prime sellers to leverage Amazon processes to enable them to be faster and more efficient.

I designed and lead a study, that involved spending 3-hours with 8 different prime sellers in their warehouses throughout the UK, working to understand their existing processes using tools such as task analysis, as well as validating an early stage prototype provided by Amazon.

THE OUTCOME

The first round of research uncovered some major flaws in Amazon’s design that we fed back to the team in Seattle. Using the insights we had uncovered, they iterated on their design and commissioned a second study to further validate their tool with users in the UK.

Bringing a new form of digital payments to India

GfK

In late 2016, the prime minister of India announced the demonetisation of two of the nations bank notes (equivalent to the £5 and £10 notes).

Visa had been developing a mobile payments app for the region, and needed to quickly validate their designs with users on the ground.

Alongside a colleague from the US office, we designed a study that included four days of RITE testing in a lab in Mumbai (India) and one day of in-context field work testing with various shop keepers in Mumbai.

THE OUTCOME

By the end of the week, the Visa design team had moved the design along and challenged some key design assumptions. A final report was delivered alongside a highlight reel of the sessions.
